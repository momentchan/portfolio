EUREKA is a real-time interactive art piece designed to create a sensory experience within a
restaurant space. The installation presents two connected visual scenes—one underwater and one on
the sea surface—where light spheres move gently through the environment.

Guest interactions in the space, such as opening the refrigerator door, quietly trigger the spheres
to rise, simulating the feeling of bubbles or shifting currents. Over time, the visuals evolve with
the atmosphere of the room, capturing the gradual rhythm of presence, motion, and changing
perception.

Rather than focusing on obvious interaction, the piece integrates input seamlessly into the
environment—allowing technology, space, and human behavior to merge into a calm, immersive
experience.
